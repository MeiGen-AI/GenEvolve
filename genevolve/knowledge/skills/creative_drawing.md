# Skill: Creative Concept & Style Transfer

## Description
This skill focuses exclusively on **conceptual transformation** — turning ordinary ideas into extraordinary, unexpected, or stylized visual concepts. It covers WHAT the concept is and HOW to transform it, NOT the technical visual execution (that's aesthetic).

- **Trigger when**: The prompt requires **style transfer** (anime, watercolor, oil painting, steampunk, cyberpunk, art nouveau), **surreal/impossible scenes** (melting objects, gravity-defying, impossible geometry), **concept blending** (combining unrelated ideas into one scene), **metamorphosis** (objects transforming into other objects), or **artistic reinterpretation** of real things into fantasy versions.
- **Do NOT trigger when**: The request is for a literal, realistic depiction with no conceptual transformation. Do NOT trigger for lighting/camera/color (→ aesthetic), object positioning (→ spatial), text (→ text_rendering), body correctness (→ anatomy), or counting (→ quantity).

---

## Instructions
When this skill is active, rewrite the user's prompt by applying the following five principles for conceptual transformation:

1. **Style Specification (Artistic Medium & Reference)**
- Name the specific art style and medium: "in the style of Studio Ghibli watercolor backgrounds," "rendered as a 1920s Art Deco poster," "as a ukiyo-e woodblock print."
- Reference specific artists or movements when appropriate: "in the style of Moebius's clean linework," "reminiscent of Dalí's melting forms."
- Specify the rendering technique: "cel-shaded," "impasto oil paint with visible brushstrokes," "pixel art at 32x32 resolution."

2. **Concept Fusion (Unexpected Combinations)**
- Identify the core subjects and find a surprising bridge between them.
- "A Victorian-era clockwork mechanism where each gear is a different living ecosystem — coral reef, rainforest, desert dunes."
- The fusion should feel deliberate and meaningful, not random.

3. **Transformation Logic (Physical Rules of the Fantasy)**
- When depicting impossible scenes, define the internal logic clearly.
- "The building is dissolving into a flock of origami cranes, starting from the rooftop and working downward, with the bottom floors still solid."
- Give the transformation a direction, a material, and a boundary.

4. **Emotional & Narrative Layer**
- Every creative concept should carry emotional weight or tell a micro-story.
- Instead of "a tree made of books," write "an ancient tree whose bark is made of weathered, yellowed book pages, with glowing words floating upward like fireflies — evoking the idea of knowledge growing and escaping into the world."

5. **Grounding the Surreal**
- Even impossible scenes need physical anchors to feel convincing.
- Describe impossible elements using real material properties: "the melting clock drips like warm honey, pooling on the marble tabletop" — not just "a melting clock."
- Contrast the surreal element with a realistic environment: "the impossible floating island hovers above a photorealistic ocean with accurate wave patterns."

## Output Format
Return ONLY the final enhanced prompt text. Do not include any conversational filler, introductory remarks, or prefixes like "Enhanced prompt:".
