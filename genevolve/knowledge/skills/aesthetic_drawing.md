# Skill: Aesthetic & Visual Technique

## Description

This skill focuses exclusively on the **technical visual execution** of an image — how light, color, camera, and composition work together to create visual impact. It does NOT cover what objects are in the scene (spatial), what the concept is (creative), or what text appears (text_rendering).

- **Trigger when**: The prompt needs specific **lighting setups** (rim light, volumetric, chiaroscuro, golden hour, neon glow), **camera/lens techniques** (telephoto, tilt-shift, macro, wide-angle, bokeh, long exposure), **color grading** (warm/cool tones, desaturated, high-contrast, split toning), or **mood/atmosphere control** (cinematic, dreamy, gritty, serene).
- **Do NOT trigger when**: The main challenge is about where objects are (→ spatial), what concept/style to use (→ creative), how many objects (→ quantity), what text to show (→ text_rendering), or body correctness (→ anatomy). If the prompt just needs "better quality" without specific lighting/camera/color needs, this skill is NOT needed.

---

## Instructions

When this skill is active, rewrite the user's prompt by integrating the following five technical visual dimensions:

1. **Lighting Design (Illumination Control)**
- Specify the type, direction, intensity, and color temperature of light sources.
- Examples: "a single warm spotlight from the upper left casting long dramatic shadows," "soft diffused overcast light with no harsh shadows," "neon blue and pink rim lights from behind the subject."
- Describe how light interacts with the scene: "volumetric light shafts through dusty air," "caustic light patterns on the wall from sunlight through a glass."

2. **Camera & Lens Technique (Optical Control)**
- Specify focal length and its effect: "telephoto compression flattening the background," "wide-angle distortion emphasizing the foreground," "macro lens with extreme shallow depth of field."
- Describe depth of field: "sharp subject with heavily blurred background bokeh," "deep focus with everything sharp from foreground to infinity."
- Specify camera angle: "low-angle shot looking up," "bird's-eye view directly overhead," "Dutch angle tilted 15 degrees."

3. **Color Palette & Grading (Chromatic Control)**
- Define the dominant color scheme: "warm amber and gold tones," "cool blue-teal desaturated palette," "high-contrast black and white with deep blacks."
- Specify color relationships: "complementary orange and blue," "analogous warm reds and yellows," "monochromatic shades of green."
- Describe post-processing feel: "film grain with faded blacks," "HDR-like vivid saturation," "muted pastel watercolor tones."

4. **Composition Framework (Visual Structure)**
- Apply specific composition rules: "rule of thirds with subject at the right intersection point," "centered symmetrical composition," "leading lines from bottom-left corner converging at the subject."
- Describe visual hierarchy: "the brightest element draws the eye first, then the eye follows the diagonal line to the secondary subject."
- Specify framing: "framed through a doorway," "negative space on the left balancing the dense subject on the right."

5. **Atmosphere & Mood (Emotional Tone)**
- Use specific sensory descriptors tied to technique: "foggy atmosphere creating visible depth layers," "rain-slicked surfaces doubling the neon reflections," "dust particles catching the backlight."
- Connect mood to technical choices: "the low-key lighting creates a mysterious noir atmosphere," "the high-key even lighting gives a clinical, sterile feel."

## Output Format
Return ONLY the final enhanced prompt text. Do not include any conversational filler, introductory remarks, or prefixes like "Enhanced prompt:".
